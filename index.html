Motivation

  • Tropical dry forests (TDF) are disappearing.
  • Concerned ecologist are investigating ways to undo the damage via restoration.
  • Native species can jump-start ecological succession of disturbed areas and aid in ecosystem function recovery.
  • Agave Angustifolia is a TDF native species. We must understand its establishment, survival and growth to determine if appropriate for restoration.

Methods

  1. One species: Agave Angustifolia.
  2. Two sites: Xochicalco and Tembembe.
  3. Seedling establishment experiment in February and April 2009.
  4. Survival, growth and fecundity at the sites from May 2009-May 2010.
  5. Germination experiment at the end of May 2010.
  6. Stage-structure demographic analysis, statistics, and simulation!

Site 1: Xochicalco

  • Near the archeological site at 1,199 m high
  • Warm and dry, summer rainfall
  • Landuse: protected

Site 2: Tembembe

  • Higher altitude
  • Less warm and dry, with less summer rainfall
  • Landuse: cattle grazing
  • Study in cattle exclusion zone

Discussion

  • No significant variance in population growth rate between sites, but higher $\lambda$ for Xochicalco.
  • Greater seed germination and establishment probabilities in Tembembe hypothetically due to lower temperatures.
  • Seedlings are difficult to observe, but other stage projections are closer to observed.
  • Stasis and growth were the most important demographic processes (increase/decrease resp. in Tembembe and conversly in Xochicalco).
  • Germination and seedling establishment were lower in Xochicalco.
  • Seed production low during 2009-2010, not a mast-seeding year.
  • $\lambda$ increases with seedling introduction.
  • $\lambda$ below 1 only if all inflorescences are removed: so mezcal is go!
